1. Purchase your domain

Get your website domain name purchased. Register your company name with .com and .net — and if you sell merchandise, consider .store and .shop. Many registrars offer package deals for all four. Purchase for a year with autopay for annual continuation (you can turn it off anytime). This keeps your startup cost low.

Anticipate copycats attempting to grab similar versions of your site. Have all domain names ready and pull the trigger together — don't jump in one at a time.

2. Set up matching email

Set your email to match your domain. @gmail allows you to transfer larger files back and forth between us. Customers also have better confidence when replies come from the same domain as your website.

3. Show me a layout you like

Share a site layout you admire — we'll lay out products and services with your own photos or videos. We can pull media from your private or public YouTube page. The code we build for your website pulls videos and photos from that location so you can rotate content without calling a developer.

A contact form for info@YourCompanyName directs all emails to your Gmail inbox so you can reply easily. This starts as a single-page setup, but we can expand: separate pages for product types, before/after projects, merchandise, vehicles, contracts, disclaimers, and payment processing. A video section with four featured clips is included.

We walk you through connecting your code to your web domain and email — then it's all yours. You review and approve before launch.
